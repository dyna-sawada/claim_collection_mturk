Overview

Thank you for taking the time to help us!
In this task, you will be presented with arguments from a series of debates. For the argument, we would like for you to identify claims made by the debater and mark them.

There are 4 steps to do this task.

  1. Read an original argument which supports the topic such as “Homework should be abolished”.
  2. Read a counter-argument against original argument.
  3. Determine claims in the counter-argument and their associated type, referred to as 'Claim Type'.
  4. Mark the sentences that states claims by selecting sentences and pushing the 'Claim Type' button.

What is a claim?

An argument consists of a claim and a premise.

  • claim: central reason given in support of the debater's stance
  • premise: additional reasons that underpin the plausibility of the claim
For example, the claim is a red sentence and the premises are blue sentences in the following argument.

Hello everyone. Today’s topic is “Homework should be abolished”. We have two points: The first point is “free time” and the second point is “decrease burden on teachers”. I will explain the first point of “free time”. We believe that if homework were to be abolished, we could have more free time. As a result, we could do more of what we really wanted like club activities, hobbies, or playing with friends. It also makes us healthier because it gives us enough time to sleep. Therefore, homework should be abolished. Thank you.

Note that in this task, a sentence that states a stance like “Therefore, homework should be abolished.” is not a claim, but rather sentences that directly supports the stance are a claim.

What is the task?

In this task, we provide two arguments: an original argument and a counter-argument against it.
Your task is to determine the following things in the counter-argument and mark them.

  1. Claims and their associated type, referred to as 'Claim Type'
  2. Restatement of the claim, if any
    3. *Note that premises are out of scope in this task.

1. Determine Claims and their associated type, referred to as 'Claim Type'

Claim Type is following 3 types in this task.

  • Value

    • Claiming that something is good or bad, important or not important.
    E.g.)

    • The most important work for students is studying.
    • Then, some instructions such as homework are very important.

  • Fact

    • Claiming that something is true or false.
    E.g.)

    • What students will do during free time is just playing games and chatting using SNS in most cases or just sleeping as they said.
    • Only a few of them can gain money through the activity.

  • Policy

    • Claiming that some action should or should not be taken.
    E.g.)

    • So we can get more free time just by reducing the amount of our homework.
    • If what they really want to do is such a 'less useful activity', they should do it current free time, for example after finishing their homework.

How to mark Claim Type

  1. Select sentences you determined as Claim
  2. Push the associated Claim Type button for marking

2. Determin Restatement of the claim, if any

In debates, the claim is sometimes restated at the end. In this case, annotate as Restatement and select the original claim.

  • Restatement

    • Paraphrase of a original claim that the amount of information doesn't change significantly.
    E.g.)

    • Original Claim: They have enough time to do their hobbies or play with friends.
    • Restatement: Student have a lot of free time than you think. At least, they have more free time than adults.

How to mark Restatement

  1. Select sentences you determined as Restatement
  2. Push the Restatement button for marking
  3. Select ID of the original claim for the Restatement

Notes

  • A claim generally consists of one or two sentences.
  • A debater might state serveral claims in a counter-argument. In that case, please mark all claims.
  • If there is a sentence that only denies the opponent's claim, the sentence states the reason for the denial is also included in a claim.
    • E.g) However, it's not true. Because the students will be more foolish by abolishing homework."

Examples

Original argument
Hello everyone. Today’s topic is “Homework should be abolished”. We have two points: The first point is “free time” and the second point is “decrease burden on teachers”. I will explain the first point of “free time”. We believe that if homework were to be abolished, we could have more free time. As a result, we could do more of what we really wanted like club activities, hobbies, or playing with friends. In my case, I go to tennis club after class until 5:00 pm and then I go to cram school until 8:00 pm. After this full day, I arrive at my home around 8:40 pm to eat dinner and take a shower. At nearly 10:00 pm I start my homework. I have a lot of homework. As a result, I go to bed late at night at nearly 1:00 am in the morning and I don’t have the opportunity to sleep for a long period of time. It is not healthy. Therefore, homework should be abolished. Thank you.
Value: something is good or bad, important or not important
Fact: something is true or false
Policy: some action should or should not be taken
Restatement: paraphrase of a original claim
Counter-argument 1
They said that students will have more free time by abolishing homework. However, what students will do during free time is just playing games and chatting using SNS in most cases or just sleeping as they said. It is because human is lazy in nature. Doing hard thing is usually difficult. Then, many students will not study at home. Of course, some students maybe study what they want to learn, however, the problem is for those kids who can’t study by themselves. Imagine, elementary school students, they don’t know what study is important and how to do that. Then, some instructions such as homework is very important. Some of you may think homework is worthless, but giving homework meaning giving the opportunity to study is much more meaningful than do nothing or doing addictive games. That's why free time by abolishing homework is rather harmful to students.
Counter-argument 2
They said that by abolishing homework, we can enjoy more free time. However, it's not true. Even if enjoying free more time is necessary, we can do so by alternative way such as using out time more efficiently. Because we can reduce time taken to do homework. For example, we can use the break between classes or time before homeroom. Moreover, we can do homework with friends during playing with them. That's why abolishing homework isn't necessary requirement to create free time.
